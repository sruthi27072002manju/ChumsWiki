Period Issues

It's important to understand the various menstrual difficulties before moving on to the various home cures. It also aids in making us aware of them so that we may assess our own cycles and decide whether to seek medical advice. These consist of,

Unusual Periods

Your periods are regarded as regular if they come every 24 to 38 days. Your periods are referred to as irregular if the interval between your periods shifts and they come earlier or later. Period irregularities can be brought on by some medications, excessive exercise, a very low or high body weight, or not getting enough calories. Hormonal imbalances might also be the cause of irregular periods.

Menstrual Cramps

When your menstrual cycle starts, your uterus may contract, causing cramps. Some women endure really painful cramps that call for medical attention.

Perimenopause Syndrome(PMS)

PMS is a condition that typically manifests as a range of mental and physical symptoms one to two weeks prior to your period. Bloating, agitation, backaches, headaches, acne, food cravings, exhaustion, anxiety, depression, stress, sleeplessness, constipation, and breast discomfort are some of the side effects it might produce.

Heavy Bleeding

Menorrhagia, or heavy periods, make you bleed more than usual. Menstruation that lasts longer than the typical five to seven days is also a possibility. The most frequent causes of menorrhagia are hormonal abnormalities, notably those involving progesterone and oestrogen. It can also be brought on by dietary and exercise changes.

Early Puberty

Precocious puberty occurs when a child's body starts transitioning into an adult one too early (puberty). Precocious puberty refers to the onset of puberty in girls before the age of eight and in boys before the age of nine. Puberty comprises the development of the body's reproductive system, rapid growth of the bones and muscles, and changes in body size and structure. Precocious puberty frequently has no known reason. Precocious puberty can occasionally be brought on by illnesses like infections, hormonal imbalances, tumours, anomalies of the brain, or accidents. Medication is frequently used as part of treatment for premature puberty in order to halt additional growth.

Symptoms

  • First period and breast development in girls
  • Boys with larger testicles and penis, facial hair, and a deeper voice
  • Underarm or pubic hair
  • Rapid expansion
  • Acne
  • Body odor in adults

Causes

Knowing the origins of puberty can help explain why certain kids experience precocious puberty. The process is kicked off by the brain producing a hormone called gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH). This hormone causes the pituitary gland, a little bean-shaped gland at the base of the brain, to produce more hormones in the testicles and ovaries of males and females, respectively (testosterone). Female sexual traits increase and develop as a result of oestrogen. Male sexual characteristics increase and evolve as a result of testosterone. Whether a child has peripheral or central precocious puberty will determine why this process starts early in some cases.

Precocious central puberty

The aetiology of this kind of early puberty is frequently unknown. Central precocious puberty is characterised by an early onset of puberty. The order and time of the process's steps are both normal. There is typically no underlying medical issue and no clear cause for the early puberty in children with this syndrome. Rarely, the following conditions may lead to central precocious puberty:

  • An epidural or spinal cord tumour (central nervous system)
  • A congenital abnormality in the brain, such as hydrocephalus (excess fluid buildup) or a benign tumour (hamartoma)
  • Radiation to the spinal cord or brain
  • Injury to the spinal cord or brain
  • McCune-Albright syndrome is a rare hereditary condition that affects the bones, skin, and hormones.
  • A category of hereditary illnesses known as congenital adrenal hyperplasia involve aberrant hormone production by the adrenal glands.
  • The condition known as hypothyroidism, in which the thyroid gland fails to generate adequate hormones

Precocious puberty on the periphery

This sort of early puberty is brought on by excess oestrogen or testosterone in your child's body. The less frequent peripheral precocious puberty takes place without the brain hormone (GnRH) that often marks the beginning of puberty. Instead, issues with the ovaries, testicles, adrenal glands, or pituitary gland are to blame for the release of oestrogen or testosterone into the bloodstream. The following factors can cause precocious puberty to develop peripherally in girls:

  • An estrogen- or testosterone-releasing tumour in the pituitary or the adrenal glands
  • McCune-Albright syndrome is an uncommon hereditary condition that affects the bones, hormones, and skin colour.
  • Exposure to lotions or ointments that contain exogenous sources of oestrogen or testosterone

    • Peripheral precocious puberty in females may also be linked to:

  • Cysts in the ovaries
  • Ovarian tumours

Risk Elements

Precocious puberty is more likely in children when:

Being a girl: Precocious puberty is substantially more common in girls.

Being obese: Precocious puberty is more likely to occur in kids who are severely overweight.

Experiencing sex hormones: The likelihood of your child experiencing precocious puberty increases if they come into touch with an oestrogen or testosterone cream, ointment, or other drugs that contain these hormones (such a prescription drug for adults or dietary supplements).

Enduring additional medical issue: Precocious puberty could result from McCune-Albright syndrome or congenital adrenal hyperplasia, two disorders that have aberrant male hormone production (androgens). Precocious puberty may in some instances also be linked to hypothyroidism.

After receiving central nervous system radiation therapy: Precocious puberty is a danger that can be heightened by radiation therapy for tumours, leukaemia, or other illnesses.

Complications

Precocious puberty's potential side effects include:

Petite height: Precocious adolescents may first grow swiftly and be tall in comparison to their peers. However, they frequently cease developing earlier than usual because their bones develop more swiftly than typical. As a result, they might grow up to be shorter than typical. When precocious puberty is treated early, especially when it affects very young children, it can assist those youngsters grow taller than they otherwise would.

Emotional and social issues: Girls and boys who reach puberty earlier than their peers may feel very self-conscious about the physical changes that are taking place. Self-esteem may be impacted, and the risk of depression or substance abuse may rise.

Preventions

Some precocious puberty risk factors, like sex and race, are unavoidable. However, there are steps you may take to lessen your child's risk of experiencing premature puberty, such as:

  • Preventing your child from coming into contact with external sources of oestrogen and testosterone, such as household prescription drugs for adults or dietary supplements containing these hormones.
  • Assisting your kid in keeping a healthy weight.
